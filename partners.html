Partner Information

The Industry Explorers program partners with organizations that share the same passion for technology and innovation. These institutions of learning provide a diverse set of candidates with a strong foundation in software development. Industry Explorers then sources from this talent pool and provides additional training. Candidates will have continuous structured training while working on real-world projects.


Partner requirements:

  • Must share a similar vision for diversity in the software industry.
  • Must provide graduates who meet the candidate qualifications.
  • Provides training in software engineering.
  • Should be willing to attend or present at events hosted by Industry Explorers.

Meet Our Partners

Ada Developers Academy

Ada Developers Academy (ADA) is a non-profit software development school whose mission is to cultivate high technical aptitude into tangible software engineering skill and employment. The program is for women who are passionate about starting a career in software and web development, but do not have a computer science degree or professional coding experience. ADA's unique year-long program begins with classroom instruction in cutting-edge web technologies. After successful completion of the in-class work students are placed in an internship with a Seattle area tech company where they learn what it's like to be part of a real engineering team shipping production code.

Kal Academy

Kal Academy is a non-profit organization passionate about inspiring women to break through barriers and pursue a career in the technology industry. Kal Academy’s founder has more than 20 years experience in the industry. She is also a distinguished faculty member distinguished faculty at CityU, Bellevue College and University of Washington. After years of teaching at local educational institutions she opened her own academy. The curriculum at Kal Academy is designed to be effective, fast and affordable while providing students with marketable technical skills. After completing the rigorous coursework students have been placed in Fortune 500 technology companies.
