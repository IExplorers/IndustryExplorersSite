Partner Information

Maximizing the contribution of every individual allows us to infuse diverse thought as a natural part of the way we innovate. To achieve this goal Microsoft has partnered with organizations that share the same vision. These non-profit academies provide the candidates with a strong foundation in software development. If selected to participate in the Industry Explorers Program, students will have three months of rigorous classroom training taught by senior Microsoft engineers. More importantly, students will have an opportunity to work on real world project(s). The projects aid the classroom learning experience by putting the students in an active role such as: software developer or program manager.

Partner requirements:

  • Partners who share a similar vision for diversity in the technology industry.
  • Organizations who provide training in disciplines such as software engineering or program management.
  • Partners must be willing to attend/present at events hosted by Industry Explorers.

Meet Our Partners

Ada Developers Academy

Ada Developers Academy is a nonprofit software development school whose mission is to develop talented women to enter the technology industry. The program is for individuals who are passionate about starting a career in software development, but do not have a computer science degree or professional coding experience. Ada's unique year-long program begins with classroom instruction in cutting-edge web technologies. After successful completion of the in-class work, students are placed in an internship with a Puget Sound tech company, where they learn what it's like to be part of a real engineering team shipping production code.
