Partner Information

Maximizing the contribution of every individual allows us to infuse diverse thought as a natural part of the way we innovate. To achieve this goal Microsoft has partnered with organizations that share the same vision. These non-profit academies provide the candidates with a strong foundation in software development. If selected to participate in the Industry Explorers Program, students will have three months of rigorous classroom training taught by senior Microsoft engineers. More importantly, students will have an opportunity to work on real world project(s). The projects aid the classroom learning experience by putting the students in an active role such as: software developer or program manager.

Partner requirements:

  • Partners who share a similar vision for diversity in the Technical Industry.
  • Organizations who provide training in disciplines such as software engineering or program management.
  • Partners must be willing to attend/present at events hosted by Industry Explorers.


Meet Our Partners

Ada Developers Academy

Ada Developers Academy (Ada) is an intensive software developer training school for women. Ada's unique year-long program begins with seven months of classroom instruction in cutting-edge web technologies in which students learn the most up-to-date development skills from leading regional and national experts. After successful completion of the in-class work, students are placed in an internship with a Puget Sound tech company, where they learn what it's like to be part of a real engineering team shipping production code.

Kal Academy

Kal Academy is a non-profit organization passionate about inspiring women to break through barriers and pursue a career in the technology industry. Kal Academy’s founder has more than 20 years in the industry. She is also a distinguished faculty member at the University of Washington. After years of teaching at local educational institutions she opened her own academy. The curriculum at Kal Academy was purposely designed to be effective, fast and affordable while still providing students with marketable technical skills. After completing the rigorous course work, students have been placed in Fortune 500 technology companies.
